Salvadorian Pupusa

A pupusa is a thick griddle cake or flatbread from El Salvador and Honduras, made with cornmeal or rice flour, similar to the Colombian and Venezuelan arepa

A pupusa is a handmade maize or rice tortilla stuffed with ingredients. Stuffing can include cheese, refried beans, squash, loroco, and chicharrón.

Ingredients for the corn dough:

  • 500 grams of cornmeal
  • 1½ cups of water (360 milliliters)

Ingredients for the refried beans

  • 250 grams of cooked beans
  • 1 piece of onion
  • 1 clove garlic
  • 2 tablespoons of oil
  • 200 grams of mozzarella cheese

Steps for the corn dough:

  1. Slowly pour the cornmeal into the warm water and mix until lumps disappear

Steps for th refried beans:

  1. Fry the onion in oil
  2. Once it is half burned, remove it and add the beans
  3. Leave it on low heat for 10 minutes and mix constantly
  4. Add the mozzarella cheese and mix

Final steps:

  1. Make a ball of dough in the palm of your hand like a golf ball and flatten it
  2. Place the refried beans in the middle and reform the ball, taking special care that the beans are enclosed in the ball and do not come out
  3. Re-flatten to a round shape
  4. Place on the griddle over medium heat
  5. When cooked on one side, flip it
  6. Serve it
